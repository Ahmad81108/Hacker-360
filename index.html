What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity involves protecting computer systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, or damage. It encompasses technologies, processes, and practices designed to safeguard devices, information, and services from unauthorized access and cyber threats.

Common Cybersecurity Threats

  • Malware: Malicious software like viruses, trojans, ransomware, designed to damage or disrupt systems.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive info by pretending to be trustworthy via email or websites.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attacks: Attackers intercept communication between users and systems.
  • Denial of Service (DoS): Overwhelming a system to make it unavailable to legitimate users.
  • Password Attacks: Attempts to steal or guess passwords to gain unauthorized access.
  • Zero-Day Exploits: Attacks that target vulnerabilities before developers have fixed them.

Best Practices to Stay Secure

  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever possible.
  • Keep software and systems up to date with security patches.
  • Be cautious with email attachments and links from unknown sources.
  • Regularly backup important data and verify the backups.
  • Use trusted security software, such as antivirus and firewalls.
  • Educate yourself and your team on recognizing cyber threats and phishing attempts.
  • Use secure networks and avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.
